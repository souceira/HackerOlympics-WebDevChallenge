Denny's Recipe

This recipe is near and dear to my heart - my mom always makes this whenever I come home for the summer or the holidays! It's my favourite dish and never fails to make me feel like I'm home.

Crispy Beef Ribs

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg beef spareribs, sliced into riblets
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 whole garlic, crushed
  • 2 pcs star anise
  • 3 pcs bay leaf
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp peppercorns
  • 3 cups water
  • cooking oil

Vinegar Dip:

  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 3 birds-eye chillies, chopped with seeds
  • 1 shallot, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • freshly ground black pepper

Instructions:

  1. Place ribs in a pot and add enough water to cover the beef, bring it to a boil and let the scum rise. Turn the heat off, drain the liquid out and rinse beef with running tap water.
  2. Place beef back into the pot together with all of the ingredients except the oil. Bring it to a boil, simmer and cook for 2 hrs. or until fork tender.
  3. Separate beef from liquid and let it cool. Once cooled down place it on paper towel lined plate to absorb excess liquid, replace paper towel if needed then place in the refrigerator for at least 4 hrs.
  4. Prepare a deep fryer or wok filled with oil, heat it up and deep fry beef ribs until crispy.
  5. Drain the ribs and place in a paper towel lined plate to drain excess oil.
  6. Prepare dip by mixing all vinegar dip ingredients, then serve with crispy ribs.

Anna's Recipe

Pesto Pasta

Ingredients

  • 1 quantity homemade pesto
  • 300 - 350 g / 10 - 12 oz pasta of choice (such as ziti, penne and spaghetti)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup pasta cooking water
  • Parmesan, for serving

Instructions
  1. Bring a large pot of water to the boil with the salt.
  2. Add pasta and cook for the length of time per the packet.
  3. Just before draining, scoop out 1 cup of of the pasta cooking water.
  4. Drain pasta in a colander, leave it for a minute.
  5. Transfer pasta to a bowl (do not use pasta cooking pot, too hot).
  6. Add pesto and 1/4 cup of pasta water. Toss to coat pasta in pesto, adding more water if required to make pasta silky and saucy, rather than dry and sticky.
  7. Taste, add more salt and pepper if desired.
  8. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parmesan.

Recipe Notes:

  1. Pesto - This recipe is intended for use with this homemade pesto which I also shared today. But the same technique applies to store bought. If using store bought, use 1/2 cup+. Usually require less than when using homemade because they have a more concentrated flavour from basil extract.
  2. Pasta - Ziti and penne are my favourites for pesto (explained in post). Spaghetti is next, then other long strand pastas.
  3. Use 300g/10oz for a really great coating of pesto sauce - 3 large servings, 4 smallish servings.
  4. Use up to 350g/12oz for "normal" amount of pesto sauce to pasta - 4 standard servings.
  5. Don't use more pasta for one batch of homemade pesto, the pasta will lack pesto flavour.

General:

  • Some people like a squeeze of lemon juice to finish!
  • Leftovers - store in fridge in super airtight container.
  • Do not reheat leftovers - will also make basil black. Just bring to room temp then consume - pesto pasta at room temp is so good!

Raisa's Recipe

Macaroni Salad :)

This is one of my favourite recipes to make for myself, my family, and for parties. I'd even consider this to my signature dish considering how many times I've been asked to bring it to potlucks and gatherings. No get together is complete without this staple. It's the perfect salad for year-round enjoyment. The best part of it is that this classic recipe is incredibly beginner-friendly. I've added my favourite ingredients down below, but feel free to make adjustments as you go according to your tastes! This recipe is very easy to customize which just adds to the fun of it all.

Ingredients!

  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced green peppers
  • 1/2 cup diced red peppers
  • 1/2 cup diced yellow peppers
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup diced cucumbers
  • 1/2 cup diced red onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley

Instructions!

  1. Boil water in a pot and add your macaroni. Cook until al dente.
  2. Drain and rinse the pasta with cold water. Make sure the pasta is fairly dry.
  3. Set aside the pasta in the fridge.
  4. In a large bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, white vinegar, salt, pepper, and sugar.
  5. Add the green peppers, red peppers, yellow peppers, carrots, cucumbers, red onions, and fresh parsley to the bowl with the dressing. Stir to combine.
  6. Add the cooled macaroni to the bowl and stir once more, tossing well for an evenly coated salad.
  7. Cover and refrigerate the macaroni salad. I prefer to leave it overnight but at least an hour or two will do as well!
  8. Enjoy your homemade macaroni salad! :)

Kalpi's Recipe

Pani Puri

One of my favourite dishes ever is Pani Puri - a very popular street dish from India. Pani-puri is also known by other names: in Gujarat (where I'm from), we call the dish "Pani Puri", but it is also known as "Paani Patashi," "Gup-chup," "Gol Gappe" (and more) in different parts of India! As it is street-food, the dish is relatively easy to make - follow these simple steps to try Pani-puri in the comfort of your own home!

Ingredients:

  • 32 Puris (ready-made)
  • 1/4 cup of Date Tamarind Chutney

Ingredients for Pani:

  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup Coriander Leaves (chopped)
  • 1-2 green chilli (chopped)
  • 1/2 inch pieces of ginger
  • 1 medium size lemon
  • 1 teaspoon Chaat Masala Powder
  • 4 cups water

Ingredients for Masala:

  • 1 1/2 cups boiled, peeled, and mashed Potato
  • 1/2 cups boiled black chickpeas
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1/2 cumin-coriander powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon chaat masala powder

Recipe:

  1. Rinse coriander and mint leaves in water and gather all ingredients needed for the "Pani" (water)
  2. Add mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilli, ginger and lemon juice in a grinder
  3. Grind mix until you have a smooth paste (if needed, add some water)
  4. Transfer your paste into a large bowl, add about 4 cups of water and stir until you have a liquid of desired consistency.
  5. Mix mashed potato, black chickpeas, red chili powder, cumin-coriander powder, and chaat masala powder together in another bowl to make your Masala
  6. Take each puri and carefully poke a hole on one side (With a spoon or your finger)
  7. Stuff the puri with masala and pour your pani mix into it
  8. Take a bite and enjoy!
